Chapter 11 Introduction

Project MinION Sequencing. Introduction

MSF-7 tumor cell line were sequenced by MinION sequencer at the ILC Research Center(HttpsCanvashunlCourses). The aim of the study was to annotate, characterize and structure transcriptome analysis of genes expressed during the development of using RNA/cDNA nanopore sequencing technology (Oxford Nanopore Technologies). Sequencing results were compared with previously obtained results of the Ying Chen from 2021 in the cancer study (Chen et al., 2021).

Five cancer cell types were sequenced and the comparative organization of gene clusters was studied. RNA sequencing using long read sequencing allows the detection of transcription at loci in the genome that are difficult to to be analyzed by short read sequencing. The aim of the Chen study was to identify the involvement of different proteins in the regulation of proliferation canerogene cells of the MSF-7, Hct116, K562, HepG2 cell lines. The purpose of this study was to obtain data on the expressed genes of tumor cells, dynamics of their transcription and polymorphism.

Studies on cancer biology with nanopore sequencing have been conducted throughout the 21st centuries, including studies on genomic variation, repeat regions, phasing, and transcript isoforms(LabX, n.d.).

Despite the available research on tumor cell function remains open the question of the mechanisms controlling cell proliferation and “escaping” from apoptotic death. Therefore, the study of molecular-genetic and transcriptional features of development of tumor cell lines is an urgent and highly demanded direction of modern research. At the same time, new technologies long-read sequencing technologies are improving the accuracy of genome annotation and detection of new transcripts, including long non-coding RNAs (dnRNAs). Therefore, the use of third-generation sequencers to
characterization of the transcriptome and the study of structural features of oncogenes is an important task, the solution of which will provide new information on the genetic basis of cancer development(Xiao & Zhou, 2020).

Third-generation sequencing provided information on genes involved in the process of tumor cell development. In addition to the complexity and polymorphism of the genes themselves involved in the tumor cell development process, their study is complicated by the genomic organization of hybrid genes, combines complex genomes, which makes annotation of its genome difficult Xiao & Zhou (2020).

A key tool for identifying genes, structural variants and characteristics is high-throughput Nanopore technology (Applications for Nanopore Sequencing, n.d.). Nanopore sequencing is a technology that allows you to direct analysis of long DNA or RNA fragments in real time, based on the change in electrical current as nucleic acids as nucleic acids pass through protein nanopores (Applications for Nanopore Sequencing, n.d.). Nanopore sequencing is used to assemble genomes, detect full-length transcripts, searching for nucleotide modifications. Applications are gaining momentum in more specialized applications, such as rapid clinical diagnostics and epidemiologic surveillance. However, in order to obtain reads of significant length, it is necessary to optimal sample preparation (Calus et al., 2018).

A prerequisite for obtaining long reads is the availability of high-quality high-molecular-weight DNA. If the start material is highly fragmented and degraded, it is not possible to obtain long reads. The methodology of isolation DNA should be carefully selected for each type of sample. The MinION is the most popular and most compact sequencer.(Lu et al., 2016)

The software decodes the sequences in real time so they can be correlated with different markers. For example, it is possible to identify a gene associated with cancer.

Project MinION Sequencing goal is to adapt and compare methods for targeted high-throughput nanopore sequencing of tumor cells, to analyze variations in MSF-7 genes and their promoters.
